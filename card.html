Lion

The lion, often called the "King of the Jungle," is a powerful and iconic big cat known for its strength, courage, and striking appearance.

  • Social Living: Lions are the only big cats that live in social groups called prides, consisting of up to 30 individuals, including females, their cubs, and a few males.
  • Powerful Roar: A lion's roar can be heard up to 5 miles away, marking their territory and communicating with the pride.
  • Majestic Mane: Male lions boast thick, dark manes that signify their age and strength, playing a key role in attracting mates.
  • Sleeping Habits: Lions rest for up to 20 hours a day to conserve energy for hunting and territorial defense.

Native to Africa and parts of Asia, lions are apex predators that play a crucial role in maintaining the balance of their ecosystems. Their social nature, roaring calls, and regal demeanor have made them a symbol of bravery and royalty throughout human history.
